Projects in Tatale, Northern Ghana

Empowerment and Development Project

This initiative trains and empowers 250 vulnerable women in the Tatale Sanguli District with sustainable livelihood skills such as soap making, beading, vegetable farming, mushroom cultivation, and beekeeping. The project aims to improve the living conditions of women, children, and families by supporting them in starting their own enterprises.

Learn More

Shea Processing Group Initiative

Supported by Tree Aid, this project involves women like Paulina Mademoube, who have been trained in shea butter production. Participants produce and sell shea butter, providing a sustainable income and improving their families' welfare.

Learn More

Clean Water Initiative by Salesian Missions

This project provided funding for water projects in Tatale, giving residents access to clean drinking water. Initiatives involved digging boreholes and providing water tanks, resulting in improved health outcomes.

Learn More

Malaria Prevention Efforts

In the Tatale-Sanguli District, malaria mortality dropped by 80% from 2018 to 2020, thanks to community leaders' involvement in prevention efforts, including distributing insecticide-treated nets and education initiatives.

Learn More

Ghana Rural Telephony and Digital Inclusion Project

Implemented in Nachamba No. 2 within the Tatale/Sanguli District, this project improves digital inclusion by providing telephony services to rural communities.
